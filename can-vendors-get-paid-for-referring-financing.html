How Vendors Often Encounter Financing Opportunities

Vendors frequently work with buyers who require financing to complete transactions. Many of these transactions rely on financing to close successfully.

Examples include:

  • Equipment vendors selling machinery
  • Truck dealers selling commercial vehicles
  • Technology vendors selling hardware or infrastructure
  • Construction equipment sellers
  • Manufacturing equipment suppliers

When a buyer cannot obtain financing through traditional channels or the vendor's in-house program, the sale may stall. Vendor financing referral programs exist to help connect those buyers with alternative financing options.

How Referral Partnerships Often Work

In commercial finance, referral partnerships are common between professionals who work with business owners and firms that arrange financing.

A typical scenario:

  • A vendor works with a customer who needs financing to complete a purchase.
  • The vendor introduces the customer to a financing advisory firm or lender network.
  • If financing successfully closes, the referring partner may receive a revenue share based on the agreement.

Structures vary by company. Compensation depends on the agreement between the referral partner and the financing firm. In many commercial finance arrangements, referral partnerships are structured through formal agreements between financing firms and referral partners.

Why Vendors Refer Financing

The main reasons vendors refer financing opportunities include:

  • Helping customers complete purchases—When a buyer needs financing to close a deal, a referral can connect them with options.
  • Preserving relationships—When customers need financing options, referring them elsewhere keeps the relationship intact instead of losing the sale.
  • Providing buyers with additional funding solutions—Vendors may not offer in-house financing for every scenario; referral partners can fill gaps.
  • Creating potential revenue share opportunities—From relationships they already maintain, vendors may earn revenue share when deals close.

Types of Financing Vendors Often Refer

Common financing types vendors may encounter include:

  • Equipment financing
  • Working capital loans
  • Business lines of credit
  • SBA loans
  • Commercial real estate financing
  • Bridge financing
  • Accounts receivable financing
  • Revenue-based financing

Different financing structures may apply depending on the business situation. Equipment-backed deals, for example, may have different credit considerations than unsecured working capital.
