Why Medical Equipment Financing Matters

Practices and facilities need capital to upgrade diagnostic equipment, add treatment capabilities, and improve patient care. Banks often restrict medical lending due to practice size, specialty, or regulatory complexity. When traditional sources decline, equipment financing through alternative lenders may create options depending on deal structure, collateral, and revenue.

Medical equipment financing through referral partners provides a path for deals that may qualify depending on structure, revenue, collateral, and lender guidelines. Common Scenarios

Situations where medical equipment financing is often sought:

  • Bank decline—Practice applied for imaging or surgical equipment and was declined for size, specialty, or credit reasons.
  • Vendor program decline—Equipment dealer's in-house program declined the buyer; alternative financing may be available.
  • Practice expansion—Clinic needs additional diagnostic or treatment equipment to serve more patients.
  • Technology upgrade—Older imaging or surgical equipment must be replaced with newer systems.
  • New practice startup—New practice needs equipment but lacks established credit history.
  • Broker lender mismatch—Deal does not fit the broker's current lender lineup.

How Medical Equipment Financing Works

Medical equipment financing operates through referral networks. A broker, equipment vendor, or advisor with a signed referral agreement submits the deal. The financing partner evaluates the opportunity and, if appropriate, matches it to a lender in their network. The referral partner does not broker the loan—they introduce the opportunity and may receive revenue share when the deal closes.

Deals are reviewed based on multiple factors: credit profile, revenue, time in business, equipment type and value, practice specialty, and structure. Opportunities may qualify depending on how these factors align with lender appetites. No approval is promised—each deal is evaluated on its merits.

FAQ

Questions about medical equipment financing

What equipment qualifies for medical equipment financing?

Diagnostic imaging (MRI, CT, X-ray), surgical equipment, patient monitors, dental equipment, lab analyzers, and other clinical devices. Lenders evaluate equipment type, useful life, and resale value. Eligibility varies by lender and program.

Why do banks decline medical equipment loans?

Banks may decline due to practice size, specialty, credit, or regulatory complexity. Alternative lenders may evaluate medical equipment deals differently based on collateral, revenue, and structure. Each deal is evaluated on its merits.

How do medical equipment vendors refer financing?

Equipment vendors with a signed referral agreement can refer buyers declined by in-house programs. Vendors introduce the opportunity; the financing partner evaluates it. Learn how vendors get paid for referring financing when deals close.

What credit do medical equipment lenders consider?

Credit requirements vary by lender. Equipment-backed deals may consider borrowers with lower credit when equipment collateral and revenue support the transaction. Approval is not guaranteed—each deal is evaluated on multiple factors.

Can dental practices get equipment financing?

Dental equipment—chairs, imaging, sterilization, and treatment units—may qualify for equipment financing depending on lender guidelines. Eligibility varies by program. Deals are evaluated on structure, revenue, and collateral.

Do I need a referral agreement to submit medical equipment deals?

Yes. Brokers and vendors who refer deals must have a signed agreement with the financing partner. The agreement defines compensation, protects both parties, and establishes the process.

How does medical equipment financing differ from other equipment types?

Medical equipment may have different useful life and resale assumptions than industrial or restaurant equipment. Lenders evaluate medical deals based on practice revenue, specialty, and equipment type. Eligibility varies by lender and program.
